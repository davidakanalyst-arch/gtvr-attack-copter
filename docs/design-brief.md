# GTVR Attack Copter Design Brief

## One-Line Target

An original compact military attack/scout helicopter for Aerofly FS 4 that keeps the EC135-like glass cockpit and friendly handling feel, but wears a much more aggressive exterior shell.

## Visual Target

The helicopter should read as modern, tactical, and compact:

- Large forward glass cockpit retained for visibility and VR comfort.
- Angular armored canopy frames around the glass rather than a full cockpit redesign.
- Narrow faceted nose with a chin-mounted sensor turret.
- Twin-engine upper fairing with guarded intakes.
- Exhaust suppressor-style side covers.
- Short stub wings with pylons.
- Visual rocket pods or sensor pods, treated as non-functional stores for the first version.
- Beefier skids.
- Matte graphite, black, or dark olive finish.

## What It Is Not

- Not a Blackhawk clone.
- Not a full real-world aircraft replica.
- Not a weapons-system simulation for the first build.
- Not a redistribution of Aerofly stock EC135 assets.

## Flight Feel

Target the EC135 feel because that is the enjoyable part:

- Stable hover.
- Smooth cyclic response.
- Useful glass cockpit.
- Comfortable in VR.
- Light twin-engine helicopter scale.

The first serious flight model should be tuned by comparison flights against the stock EC135 rather than by chasing real-world attack-helicopter specs.

## First Playable Definition

A useful first in-sim milestone is:

- Appears in the Aerofly FS 4 aircraft menu as `GTVR Attack Copter`.
- Loads at a helipad without errors.
- Has visible custom exterior geometry.
- Keeps glass cockpit readability.
- Main rotor and tail rotor animate.
- Uses EC135-like mass, rotor, and control behavior as a tuning target.
- Includes one matte tactical livery.
